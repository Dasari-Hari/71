school library

Was build in 1954

The school provides a well-stocked library containing nearly 10,000 books, to set high standard of excellence in academics. Besides inculcating reading habit in them, it is a boon to the students for reference work and projects. Various magazines, news-papers and periodicals are provided to update students on current events. All library transactions are computerized. The well-equipped library serves to quench the thirst for knowledge among the students. Fictional as well as reference books can be retained for days. Only fiction or reference books may be borrowed at a time. Reference books viz. encyclopedias, dictionaries etc are to be used only within the library. Books and magazines are issued for a week. Approximately 15 types of magazines are available for the erudition of the students. Each borrower is held responsible for all books taken by her/him and for all fines occurring on the same. No books may be taken from the library without the permission of the librarians. All the Librarians that attract a vast number of students to the library provide exemplary service.

school PLAYGROUND

Was build in 1954

For the all-round development of students emphasis is also laid on co-curricular activities. Here we believe that a healthy mind resides in a healthy body. Physical training games and sports are compulsory activities on the school curriculum. The students are provided with suitable facilities to display their sporting skills. Several playgrounds and courts have been constructed within the premises of the school. Centrally located basket ball court attracts several aspiring sporting stars. There is a special ground for kho-kho. Under the guidance of the games masters the school has achieved many accolades in the field of sports .

steel City of Jamshedpur in the field of education
The year 2004 is a milestone in the history of Church School as it celebrates fifty Golden Years of service to the steel City of Jamshedpur in the field of education.

Sir Jahangir Ghandy, Resident Director of TISCO requested 'The Roadys' to add one or two classes every year for the children of TISCO officials .The year 2004 is a milestone in the history of Church School as it celebrates fifty Golden Years of service to the steel City of Jamshedpur in the field of education. Church School, Beldih established in the year 1954 is situated at Beldih Triangle, Circuit House Area, Jamshedpur. The missionaries established the Beldih Baptist Church in the year 1923. The School was established with able support from the Church and has been managed thence by the Executive Management Committee of the Church.

In the first couple of decades of the 19th century
In the first couple of decades of the 19th century, the Americans, British and Europeans comprising executives, engineers, administrators, designers, planners and technocrats along with their families started arriving in Jamshedpur.

Lured by the prospects of Jamshedpur hundreds and thousands from all over India migrated to Jamshedpur. A large section of incoming Christians belonged to the Baptist faith speaking different languages – Bengali, Marathi, Oriya, Punjabi and Telugu etc. They adopted Hindi as the mode of communication .The land for the Church building and parsonage was acquired from TISCO .A small Kindergarten was started by 'The Roadys' in the parsonage to look after the education of the little children of the American families.

Interact is a Rotary - Sponsored service club that gives young people an opportunity to participate in fun and meaningful service projects.. While developing leadership skills and meeting new friends. The basic principle of the Rotary Interact is to help others. It's Motto is 'service above Self'. Interact Club of Church School Beldih was established in 1988 under the able leadership of its Moderator.. Moitreyee Bhattacharjee. Later Mrs Rashmi Jhingran shouldered the responsibilities. Mrs Ameeta Sinha took over the reins after her retirement. At present the Club is doing excellent projects under her guidance..
